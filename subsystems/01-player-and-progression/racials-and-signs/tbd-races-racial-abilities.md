## Design Philosophy

**Core Principles:**
1. **Meaningful Differences** - Each race offers distinct mechanical advantages and playstyles
2. **Lore Authenticity** - Abilities reflect Elder Scrolls lore and cultural backgrounds
3. **Starting Specialization** - Races encourage specific builds through starting skill bonuses
4. **Permanent Choice** - Race selection is permanent and defines long-term character identity
5. **Balanced Diversity** - No strictly superior race; each excels in different areas

**Influences:**
- Morrowind (Highly differentiated races with major mechanical differences)
- Requiem (Significant racial bonuses that define playstyle)
- Classic D&D (Race-based ability score modifiers and special abilities)
- Elder Scrolls Lore (Authentic racial characteristics from across the series)

## Race Overview

Tamriel is home to **10 playable races**, divided into three major categories:

### Men (Humans)
- **Bretons** - Half-elven mages from High Rock
- **Imperials** - Cosmopolitan warriors and diplomats from Cyrodiil
- **Nords** - Nordic warriors of Skyrim
- **Redguards** - Desert warriors from Hammerfell

### Mer (Elves)
- **Altmer (High Elves)** - Magical supremacists from Summerset Isle
- **Bosmer (Wood Elves)** - Archer-thieves from Valenwood
- **Dunmer (Dark Elves)** - Ash-skinned sorcerers from Morrowind
- **Orsimer (Orcs)** - Battle-hardened smiths from Orsinium

### Beast Races
- **Argonians** - Reptilian alchemists from Black Marsh
- **Khajiit** - Feline thieves and traders from Elsweyr

## Detailed Racial Profiles

### 1. Argonian

**Homeland:** Black Marsh
**Archetype:** Stealth Alchemist, Swamp Guerrilla
**Philosophy:** "Masters of land and water, poison and shadow"

#### Starting Attributes
- **Health:** 140 (High)
- **magic:** 120 (Medium)
- **Stamina:** 100 (Low)
- **Carry Weight:** 200
- **Speed Bonus:** 0%
- **Unarmed Damage:** 32 (High - claws)

#### Regeneration Rates
- **Health:** 0.20/s (boosted by Histskin to 1.20/s)
- **magic:** 1.10/s
- **Stamina:** 1.60/s

#### Starting Skills
- **One-Handed:** 10
- **Evasion:** 10
- **Sneak:** 10
- **Wayfarer:** 10
- **Alchemy:** 15 (Best)
- **Restoration:** 10
- **Destiny:** 1

#### Racial Abilities

**Waterbreathing**
- Breathe underwater indefinitely
- Swimming speed +20%
- Swimming stamina cost -75%
- *Application:* Underwater exploration, swamp combat, escaping via water

**Claws**
- Unarmed damage +20 (total 52 damage)
- *Application:* Monk builds, early game survival

**Histskin**
- Health regeneration +1/s (total 1.20/s)
- Potions +10% effectiveness and duration
- *Application:* Sustain tank, alchemy specialist

**Resist Poison and Disease**
- Poison damage -75%
- Disease resistance +75%
- *Application:* Swamp survival, fighting venomous creatures

**Strong Stomach**
- Can eat raw food without food poisoning
- *Application:* Wilderness survival

#### Best Builds
- **Swamp Assassin** - Stealth + Alchemy (poison daggers)
- **Argonian Monk** - Unarmed + Evasion + Histskin regen
- **Guerrilla Fighter** - Hit-and-run tactics with underwater escapes
- **Alchemist Archer** - Poison arrows + stealth

#### Strengths
- Unmatched underwater combat and exploration
- Best poison resistance in the game
- Strong alchemy synergy
- Decent starting health pool

#### Weaknesses
- Low starting stamina
- No magic bonuses (despite medium pool)
- Limited combat skill diversity

## Race Selection Strategy

### Choose Based on Playstyle

**Pure Warrior Builds**
- **Best:** Nord (melee boost), Orsimer (tank), Redguard (one-handed)
- **Viable:** Imperial (balanced)

**Pure Mage Builds**
- **Best:** Altmer (highest magic), Breton (magic resist)
- **Viable:** Dunmer (destruction focus)

**Thief/Stealth Builds**
- **Best:** Khajiit (thief bonuses), Argonian (stealth + alchemy)
- **Viable:** Bosmer (archer), Dunmer (nightblade)

**Hybrid Builds**
- **Best:** Imperial (balanced), Dunmer (battlemage)
- **Viable:** Breton (spellsword), Redguard (stamina hybrid)

**Specialist Builds**
- **Archer:** Bosmer (armor pen)
- **Monk:** Khajiit or Argonian (unarmed damage)
- **Dragonborn:** Nord (shout bonuses)
- **Alchemist:** Argonian (potion effectiveness)

## Balance Considerations

### Power Tiers

**Early Game (Levels 1-20)**
- **Strongest:** Khajiit (thief bonuses), Argonian (claws), Nord (health)
- **Weakest:** Altmer (fragile), Breton (low physical stats)

**Mid Game (Levels 20-50)**
- **Strongest:** Imperial (regen sustain), Redguard (stamina), Dunmer (versatile)
- **Weakest:** Altmer (still fragile without perks)

**Late Game (Levels 50+)**
- **Strongest:** Altmer (spell power scaling), Breton (magic resist + absorption)
- **Weakest:** Races without scaling bonuses

**Challenge Difficulty**
- **Easiest:** Orsimer (tankiest), Imperial (most forgiving)
- **Hardest:** Altmer (glass cannon), Bosmer (fragile archer)

## Summary

Races in this system are **permanent, meaningful choices** that:
- Define starting power level and attribute distribution
- Encourage specific playstyles through bonuses and starting skills
- Reflect Elder Scrolls lore authentically
- Create diverse build opportunities across 10 distinct races
- Scale differently throughout the game (some peak early, others late)

**The goal:** Make players say "I'm playing an Argonian alchemist" or "I'm a Nord dragonborn" and have that choice fundamentally shape their character identity and gameplay experience.
